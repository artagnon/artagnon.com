Here, we do exercises from Tao's excellent book on Analysis

$\S$ Define set equality, and verify that it is `reflexive`, `symmetric`, and `transitive`.

First, let us informally define sets and precisely define set membership $\epsilon$. A set is an unordered collection of `objects`, in which the set itself is an object. Set membership is defined as:

$$ \epsilon : \textbf{Obj} \rightarrow \textbf{Obj} \rightarrow \mathbb{P} $$

Equality is defined as:

$$ \begin{matrix} A = B \rightarrow &\forall x \in A, x \in B \\ &\wedge \\ &\forall x \in B, x \in A \end{matrix} $$

Goals:

$$ \begin{matrix} \textrm{reflexive} & \forall A, A = A \\ \textrm{symmetric} & \forall A B, A = B \leftrightarrow B = A \\ \textrm{transitive} & \forall A B C, (A = B) \wedge (B = C) \rightarrow (A = C) \end{matrix} $$

Proofs:

$$ \begin{matrix} A = A & \rightarrow & \forall x \in A, x \in A \rightarrow \top & \textrm{by exact}. \\ A = B & \rightarrow & \forall x \in A, x \in B & \\ && \wedge & \\ && \forall x \in B, x \in A & \\ & \leftrightarrow & B = A & \textrm{by swapping the arguments of } \wedge. \\ A = B &&& \\ \wedge &&& \\ B = C & \rightarrow & \forall x \in A, x \in B & \textrm{(1)} \\ && \wedge & \\ && \forall x \in B, x \in A & \textrm{(2)} \\ && \wedge & \\ && \forall x \in B, x \in C & \textrm{(3)} \\ && \wedge & \\ && \forall x \in C, x \in B & \textrm{(4)} \\ & \rightarrow & \forall x \in A, x \in C & \textrm{by (1) and (3)} \\ && \wedge & \\ && \forall x \in C, x \in A & \textrm{by (4) and (2)} \\ & \rightarrow & A = C & \textrm{by exact}. \end{matrix} $$

$\S$ Using the above definition of equality, the empty set axiom, and the pairwise union axiom, prove that the following sets are distinct: $\phi, \{\phi\}, \{\{\phi\}\}, \{\phi, \{\phi\}\}$.

Hypothesis:

$$ \begin{align} A = B \rightarrow (\forall x \in A, x \in B) \wedge (\forall x \in B, x \in A) \tag 1 \\ \forall x, x \not\in \phi \tag 2 \\ x \in A \cup B \leftrightarrow x \in A \vee x \in B \tag 3 \end{align} $$
